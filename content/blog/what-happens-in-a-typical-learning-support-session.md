One of the biggest questions parents ask is, "What exactly happens during a learning support session?" The answer? A lot more than just tutoring.

Unlike general academic help, learning support therapy targets *how* your child learns. Each session is tailored to a child's unique profile — what they struggle with, how they process information, and what support unlocks their best self.

Here's what you can typically expect:

- **Warm-Up and Connection**: We begin with a short, relationship-building activity that helps the child feel safe and seen.
- **Focused Skill Work**: Based on their learning goals, we dive into executive functioning, literacy or numeracy tasks, memory strategies, or emotional regulation.
- **Multisensory Techniques**: Hands-on, visual, auditory, and kinesthetic strategies engage the whole brain — not just the textbook side.
- **Confidence Boosting Moments**: Every session ends with a success — a moment where the child recognizes their progress and leaves empowered.

The best sessions are those where a child feels supported, challenged, and understood. It's not about perfection — it's about progress.
